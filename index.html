Enforcing Uniform Dress Code In Schools

Dress code has been a continous problem in schools. It has raised concerns regarding socioeconomic status, self expression, and how well the school preforms when enforcing dress codes.

Continuous Problems:

  1. Bullying due to socioeconomic background

    2. There hae been incidents where a scholar coming from a lower background has affected them by being bullied, due to not having the up to date fashion of clothes nowadays.

  2. School preformance is affected

    3. There have been recent statistics proving that schools preformances in enrollment have declined, and in school ranking as well due to not being seen as " Professional enough " regarding students showing up to school in inappropriate clothing. Enforcing Dress code could increase the school's ranking, and enrollement statistcs for deeming as a professional style in the school community.

  3. A students self expression can lead to a decrease in their self confidence

    4. Recent studies prove that a students self esteem does decrease when they see they are not like other students who have up to date clothes, or the brand new jordans that came out the day before. It could be for many reasons, but still proves that a students self expression is affected.

In conclusion, it is proven that school dress code is a big problem in our community these days, and it needs to be solved right away.

How can we solve this?

  • Have school policy makers and educators create a uniform policy if they don't already have one, or enforce the current policy they have everyday to keep it in check.
  • Form a student committee to provide input on dress code policies, ensuring that students feel represented and respected.

How does this benefit the school and others?

  • Having a dress code can instill a sense of professionalism and preparedness among students, preparing them for future workplaces where certain attire may be expected.
  • Following a dress code can instill a sense of discipline and respect for the school environment, reinforcing the importance of rules and guidelines.
  • By reducing visible differences in socioeconomic status, dress codes can help create a more inclusive environment where all students feel equal.
